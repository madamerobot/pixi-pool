Cafe Robot

If you like Pizza, you will never starve in Amsterdam. Almost every day a week there is a Tech-Meetup that gives away free and greasy deliciousness alongside Nerd-talks. I didn't want to do the same.

I wanted to create a Tech-Meetup that is inclusive for any sort of experience level and where people can ask all the 'dumb' questions they are normally afraid of. I decided to create the 'first non-awkward Tech-Meetup', and Cafe Robot was born.

Cafe Robot is a community for newbie-developers and code-hobbyists in Amsterdam. We usually meet once a month to discuss ideas and questions and to have 1-3 drinks. If you want to stay in touch, sign up for our newsletter and check out our page for more info.

Human Centered Development

While teaching at Digital School Hyper Island I developed the concept of 'Human Centered Development' which I firmly believe in and follow in my day-to-day work.

'Human Centered Development' takes inspiration from IDEOs 'Human Centered Design' approach and maps it towards the tech-industry. At the core of this concepts sits the following paradigm: "Technology is a tool to build a solution to a problem. Technology is not the the solution."

As I see the user at the center of any technical implementation, I find it very important to step back from 'RDD (Resume Driven Development)' but rather invest time into understanding the users or clients needs.

This can be achieved in several ways. When working on technical problems, I personally use a lot of communication to clarify on use-cases, eliminate assumptions and find the most economical and time-efficient solution.

Wikipedia defines 'Web Technology' as 'Communication between computers'. In my opinion it should rather be 'Communication between humans on computers'.
